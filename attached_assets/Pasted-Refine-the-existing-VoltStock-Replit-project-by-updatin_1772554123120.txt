Refine the existing VoltStock Replit project by updating both the **Dashboard** and **Inventory** experience into one unified, modern, operational UI/UX system.

Do not restart the app.
Do not rebuild from scratch.
Extend the current codebase and preserve the existing architecture where possible.

The goal is to make the app feel like a serious internal inventory platform for a **U.S. electrical contractor / electrical materials company**, with a cleaner and slightly darker modern UI direction.

---

## Overall Product Direction

This app should feel:

* modern
* operational
* visually intuitive
* clean and premium
* slightly darker and more grounded than the current bright version
* appropriate for warehouse, purchasing, project management, and field inventory use

Do not make it a fully dark mode app.
Instead, use a **slightly darker professional tone**:

* light charcoal / blue-gray accents
* deeper card contrast
* soft shadows
* muted backgrounds
* stronger hierarchy for important panels
* still readable and business-friendly

The result should feel like a premium B2B dashboard, not a generic starter template.

---

## Main UX Principle

The Dashboard and Inventory pages must work together as one system:

* **Dashboard** = operational control center
* **Inventory** = detailed browsing and lookup workspace
* **Category Detail** = fast category-specific stock comparison by size

Users should be able to:

* understand inventory health within 5 seconds
* identify shortages quickly
* jump into the correct category in 1–2 clicks
* compare size-based stock levels easily
* move naturally between Dashboard, Inventory, Reorder, and Transactions

#### B. Action Required

Create a strong action panel that highlights:

* out-of-stock items
* critical low-stock items

#### C. Top Low Stock Items

* SKU

* quantity left

#### D. Recent Transactions

Keep a recent transaction panel showing:

* item

* quantity
* timestamp

#### E. Inventory Overview

Include at least one visual summary such as:

* inventory by category

Use charts only where helpful and keep them clean.
